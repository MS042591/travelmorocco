Rabat is a city of quiet dignity and modern sophistication. As the administrative capital and the primary residence of the King, it is impeccably maintained. It offers a unique mix of 12th-century Almohad history and 20th-century French colonial urbanism.

The Kasbah of the Oudayas, with its blue and white streets and stunning views over the Atlantic and the Bou Regreg river, is a highlight. Rabat is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a must-see for those who appreciate history without the chaos of the larger cities.
